## **Pattern Recognition as Tarot Superpower**

## 

**|FNAME|**, your **|TYPE_ADJ|** mind needs the full picture before it trusts.

I learned why the hard way—with partially dislocated shoulders and six weeks of unnecessary pain.

I remember when I first started training aerial under a coach who was an incredible performer, but weirdly enough, they hated teaching.

I was so excited about learning new things that **I forgot to map the complete system.**

**I had incomplete information: certain moves weren't supposed to hurt.**

I accidentally trained aerial silk, 4 days a week, on partially subluxed shoulders for **6 entire weeks.**

All because **I didn't know which questions to ask.**

My shoulders kept coming out of socket, but I loved aerial so much that I dealt with the pain and kept training and icing like a mad woman.

I would visit my insanely patient chiropractor twice a week and he would say something along the lines of "you should really consider stopping aerial because your shoulders keep coming out" or "take a break."

**But I knew stopping wasn't the answer—I just didn't understand what variable I was missing.**

It was only when a physical therapist who specializes in aerialists walked into my life that **the pattern finally revealed itself.**

**I wasn't fully engaging my shoulders and upper back.**

My shoulders were just hanging there!

I thought it was proper form because I was replicating what I'd been shown!

My coach wasn't offering body mechanics information, and **I didn't have enough framework to recognize the gap in my understanding.**

I worked with my PT for months and **discovered the missing structural element:** So long as I engaged my shoulders and upper back when I hung from the silks, my shoulders remained in their sockets.

**It was the difference between replicating movement and understanding the architecture beneath it.**

My chiropractor was relieved, my shoulders began to heal and strengthen, and my form rapidly improved.

Best of all? I didn't have to go to sleep with ice packs on my shoulders every night anymore.

**This experience taught me something critical: Seeking to understand the underlying mechanics doesn't just satisfy curiosity—it reveals the structure that keeps you safe and effective.**

And how does this relate to tarot?

**It's all about building the complete framework.**

### **3 Variables to Map Tarot: Unlock the Algorithm**

And here’s how you can map tarot.  All cards can be grouped into 3 variables that will guide you through, step-by-step, the undulating terrain— unlock the underlying algorithm—that is reading a tarot divination:
- 

**Numerical sequences become stories.** Aces are seeds—pure potential, the spark. Tens are completion—the culmination, the harvest. Everything between is the journey from beginning to end. You don't memorize this. You recognize it as narrative arc.
- 

**Suits become domains.** Each suit handles one area of life with consistent internal logic. Wands = action domain. Cups = emotion domain. Swords = thought domain. Pentacles = material domain. Same structural principle applied to different contexts.
- 

**Court progressions become developmental stages.** Page learns. Knight acts. Queen masters internally. King masters externally. You see this pattern once, and suddenly 16 "confusing" court cards become a simple 4×4 matrix.

Once you see these patterns, 78 cards become frameworks. The whole system collapses into something your mind can hold without strain.

You don't memorize, you deduce.

"Seven of Pentacles. Seven is the assessment stage—taking stock, evaluating progress. Pentacles is the material domain—resources, work, physical realm. So this is probably about evaluating your work, checking in on material progress."

And you'd be right.

New spreads make immediate sense because they're just spatial logic—positions with geometric relationships.

You spot contradictions in readings that others miss because you're tracking internal consistency.

You build your own meanings from visual cues when guidebooks don't match what you see.

Your readings have coherent narrative arcs instead of random card fragments strung together — and if you’re not reading yet, this is what they’ll become. 

All because you have a strong foundation of visual logic. Let me tell you…
