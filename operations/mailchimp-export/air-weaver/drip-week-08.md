## **Tarot Unlocked: The Body as Structural Diagram**

Last week: your eyes process images 11x times faster than words.

This week: your nervous system already speaks the language those images are using.

*|FNAME|*, you don't need to memorize what body positions mean. You already know.

Let me show you what I mean.

Body language is something that every human understands. 

Think of dancers. We can see their emotion with how they move.

Your body understands this unspoken language because we all have the same hardware; the nervous system. 

I’m reminded of one of my dear friends' performance journey. 

When they first started performing regularly, you could see in their body how timid they really were.

No eye contact to the audience, shoulders slightly rolled forward, and they moved much faster in performance than in practice. 

This slowly evolved over time to chest open, eyes locking with audience members, and perfectly in time intentional movements.

She went from beginner, to professional after years of practice and repetition.

It has been an honor to watch.

The audience can tell when someone is afraid of them vs when someone is electrified with their attention. 

And this translates directly to our cards.

The art in our cards are still images. Frozen moments. And yet, the meaning moves.

It flows through gesture, radiates from posture, pulses in the space between figures.

What if the body, yours and the bodies in the cards, already knows what the mind is still trying to figure out?

### **Why Bodies Communicate Structure**

Physical positioning reveals internal state. Body language = visible psychology.

You already know this. Someone crosses their arms and steps back—you read defense. Someone leans forward with open palms—you read engagement. You don't think about it. Your body reads their body automatically.

Tension patterns show where energy is blocked or active. Clenched jaw, rigid shoulders, tight fists—you see held energy, resistance, strain. Relaxed limbs, flowing movement, open chest—you see available energy, receptivity, ease.

Balance/imbalance immediately registers as stable/unstable. A figure standing solidly on both feet reads differently than someone teetering on one leg or leaning precariously. Your body feels the difference viscerally.

Direction of movement indicates trajectory. Approaching, withdrawing, ascending, descending—these aren't just actions, they're vectors showing where energy is going.

Your body reads other bodies automatically through mirror neurons and somatic empathy. You feel what you see. This is evolutionary wiring.

### **Physical States as Tarot Meanings**

**Suspension** = being held between states, pause, voluntary sacrifice, seeing from inverted perspective (The Hanged Man)

**Collapse** = overwhelm, defeat, the moment of complete surrender, end of struggle (Ten of Swords)

**Reach** = desire, aspiration, grasping toward something just out of touch, longing (Four of Cups)

**Grounding** = stability, physical presence, connection to material reality, rooted strength (Pentacles suit energy)

**Ascension** = rising energy, ambition, spiritual reaching, upward trajectory (Wands suit energy)

The PHYSICAL STATE is the meaning. Words just describe it.

These physical positions create geometric patterns in space—angles, curves, lines of force. Sacred geometry isn't just symbolic. It's the actual structural relationship between body, gravity, and the forces acting upon it.

### **How Movement Reveals What Stillness Hides**

Static images show one frozen moment. But cards represent PROCESSES—transitions, journeys, changes over time.

Movement shows three things simultaneously: the path IN to this state, the current state itself, and the path OUT of this state.

Example: The Fool isn't just playing his piccolo. He’s playing in a NEW way, suspended in a lyra— in a moment of faith, the transition from known to unknown, the active choice to leave the Earth. A static image suggests this. Movement would show it.

Example: Death isn't just a skeleton on a pole stage. Her MOTION upward is transformation—the unstoppable approach, the transitional process, the active force of change. The image hints at movement. Actual movement would demonstrate the force.

Your structural mind wants to see the forces, not just the snapshot.

### **The Bridge to Three-Dimensional Understanding**

Flat cards suggest depth but can't show it.

They hint at tension but can't make it visible. They imply balance but can't demonstrate actual gravitational forces. They reference suspension but can't create real suspended states.

What if you could see the APPARATUS creating the card's structure? The actual rigging holding suspension. The real tension in fabric bearing weight. The sacred geometry formed by body and equipment in three-dimensional space—not symbolic, but structural.

What if tension, balance, suspension weren't suggested by illustration—but literally visible as physical reality?

What if the geometry wasn't implied—it was BUILT?

We're going there next week.
