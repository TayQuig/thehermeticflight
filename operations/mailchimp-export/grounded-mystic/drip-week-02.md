## **When Integration Becomes Avoidance**

## 

*|FNAME|*, last week I told you that your *|TYPE_ADJ|* approach isn't broken.

That's true. But it's not the whole truth.

Every gift casts a shadow. And if I'm going to earn your trust over these weeks, I need to show you I understand both sides.

Let me tell you about the time my own need for "useful magic" almost cost me the mystery entirely.

During the 2020 Pandemic, I had so much time on my hands.

Who didn't?

I discovered Reiki and began my training soon after the shutdowns began. I fell in love with the meditations, the symbols, the stories about Usui and how he discovered this practical magic.

I integrated the practice so fully that Reiki became a way of living for me and my family.

It still is to this day.

After a couple of years of practice, a few individuals asked me to teach them.

Even after practicing for two entire years with well over 100 clients, I felt like I had no business sharing what I knew. Two years felt insufficient.

In spite of wanting to teach, I felt as though I didn't know enough.

I sought mentors, became a Reiki master, continued my research, even explored other Reiki modalities.

For literal years, I allowed caution to hold me back from the natural expansion of my journey.

I feared stagnation.

My nervous system knew it.

Here's the tension: curiosity is a gift.

Until it becomes a shield.

There's a shadow that lives in the seeker's heart, and it deserves honest acknowledgment: the fear that if you claim what you know, you'll stop being the seeker. That if you anchor somewhere, you'll lose permission to keep searching.

Both realities can exist.

Allow me to show you how.

### **The Utilitarian Trap**

You encounter pure mystery and your first instinct is: "But what's the practical application?"

Someone shares a dream that shook them to their core. You immediately start translating it into actionable insights. A friend describes a mystical experience that defies explanation. You find yourself trying to ground it, categorize it, make it useful.

You can't let anything just BE without needing it to DO something.

But not every mystical experience translates to a to-do list. Not every spiritual truth needs an action item. Sometimes magic loses its power when you force it into practical frameworks too quickly.

The Divine doesn't always come with instructions. The numinous doesn't owe you utility.

### **Balance as Avoidance**

Staying in the middle can mean never committing to either edge.

When you use "integration" to avoid going deep into pure intuition OR pure logic, both/and becomes neither/nor. You float in the safe middle distance, never surrendering fully to mystery and never committing completely to rigorous analysis.

Sometimes the truth demands you choose a side temporarily. Dive deep into the mystical without needing to surface with practical proof. Or commit fully to logical analysis without apologizing for leaving magic out of it for a moment.

Real integration means being able to inhabit each pole fully before bringing them together—not hovering in lukewarm middle ground.

### **The Cost of Over-Translation**

Your gift is bridging spiritual to practical. But constantly translating can exhaust the mystery.

Some truths lose power when made too concrete. The Hanged Man's wisdom of voluntary suspension doesn't always need to become "schedule contemplation time this Thursday." The High Priestess's knowledge of the unseen doesn't always need to be explained or justified.

Your need to ground everything can trap others in your framework. They came to you for mystery—don't rob them of it by making everything actionable before they've had time to simply experience it.

And that brilliant integrative mind that serves you so well? It can also become a cage. The intellect that bridges worlds can also prevent you from fully entering either one.

### **The Edge You Need**

This week, notice when you reach for "practical application" automatically.

What happens if you let one mystical experience remain unexplained? What if you pull a card and sit with the mystery without translating it into next steps? What if you let magic be magic without needing it to be useful?

Just once. See what that feels like.

Your integration gift doesn't disappear when you stop using it for a moment. But you might discover what you've been avoiding by always staying balanced.

The shadow isn't comfortable. That's how you know you're actually looking at it.
