## **A Deck Built on Integration**

*|FNAME|*, last week I showed you the concept.

Seventy-eight cards of professional aerial performers. Sacred geometry you can see. Spiritual truth demonstrated in physical form.

This week, let me show you what that means for your actual practice.

Because every element of this deck was designed for the way you already think.

Through my journey as an aerialist, I have found that sequences and movements translate throughout all the different apparatuses I have experimented with.

I began on aerial silk and studied that for an entire year before branching out to lyra and pole.

I had created what I thought was a solid foundation of understanding before exploring more.

After I started lyra, I discovered an entire world of understanding I had hardly tapped into with just my silks experience.

I saw how sequencing on silks carried over to lyra.

I felt familiar movements translate.

I felt my brain unlock another layer of how to move and combine poses. It expanded my ability as an aerialist not just on the new apparatus, but on the one I had previously studied.

I found silk to be light and flowing while lyra is sturdy and dynamic.

I discovered my gymnastics background supporting my silks journey; I could use my strength to find poses that directly carried through to poses in the lyra.

I discovered this same power while moving between poses on the silks.

It wasn't about holding a pose. It was the movement from one pose to the next that mattered most.

This translated so beautifully into lyra because of the potential to spin so much faster.

The way a pose unfurls between movements is like watching a bud blossom.

It is as stunning on lyra as it is on silk when the aerialist embodies this exploratory energy.

This same concept aids our journey with tarot divination: we're not just seeing meanings of the cards. We get to see the movement within the aerialist, the energy they embodied, the energy between the cards, and the movement of energy from one card to another.

You've spent weeks discovering that tarot rewards curiosity. That questions are more valuable than certainty. That exploration is the practice.

When the mystical and the practical integrate, the learning never stops.
