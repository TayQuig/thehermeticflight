## **Sacred Geometry You Can Touch**

*|FNAME|*, for eight weeks I've been speaking to your *|TYPE_ADJ|* mind.

Integration. Both/and thinking. Ancient wisdom grounded in practical reality.

Now I want to show you what I built.

What if the mystical and the tangible were finally integrated in every single card? What if there was a deck that refused to make you choose?

Recently, I discovered there was a critical gap in my understanding of aerial arts.

I had learned a certain set of moves on the silk and felt like I'd hit a ceiling; like I'd mapped everything my current framework could support.

Yet I could observe that other women in my age group were excelling and executing moves of great difficulty.

I wasn't accessing those movement patterns yet; I couldn't identify what variable I was missing.

All I could do was try not to compare myself because I honestly thought my system simply had different limitations.

It wasn't until I left my studio and took classes at a new place that I realized I was literally missing foundational information that would unlock the progression I was trying to map.

In a previous email I talked about how my shoulders had been an issue because I never knew how to actually use them in a healthy way in aerial.

This information gap I'm talking about is something most aerial studios systematically teach: proper body mechanics and conditioning that supports aerial movement.

Where it had been drilled into me—"you have to condition condition condition" and "stretch all of the time"—I didn't have the complete framework for how to do these things effectively.

Even with my decade of gymnastics, running fitness groups, and yoga training.

Aerial moves your body in ways nothing else does.

It's a unique mechanical system.

And in going to a new studio, very suddenly, this mysterious progression I'd been trying to decode made complete sense.

I had a roadmap to get there.

All the disconnected pieces of information started integrating, and my practice accelerated.

I discovered new pathways on silk; different combinations in lyra.

I was also able to DECODE this information in accomplished aerialists' performances.

The mystery of how to progress dissolved because I could now map the underlying structure I was observing.

So we built this concept directly into our cards.

What if the Eight of Swords wasn't an illustration of feeling trapped—what if you could see actual binding? Real tension embodied? The precise moment of constriction?

What if court cards weren't drawings of personalities but actual bodies demonstrating developmental stages in space?

What if the sacred geometry of tarot wasn't symbolic... but structural?

The mystical becomes grounded when you can see how it works.

### **The Hermetic Flight Concept Revealed**

Seventy-eight cards of professional aerial performers.

Each card = one or more performers on aerial apparatus. Silks, lyra, rope, straps, trapeze, hammock, pole. And fire flow for the Torches suit.

The apparatus creates literal sacred geometry in three-dimensional space. Not suggested. Not illustrated. Actually built. Real structures you can see formed by real bodies working with real physics.

Body positioning demonstrates card meaning through physical structure. Not artistic interpretation—embodied demonstration. Ancient wisdom made visible and tangible simultaneously.

This is the deck that refuses to make you choose between beauty and function, mystery and clarity, art and wisdom.

Both. Always both. Integrated at the foundation.

### **Why Aerial Arts Serve Integration**

**Tension:** Not implied—visible. You see how fabric holds weight, how bodies work against gravity, where strain concentrates. The Eight of Swords shows actual binding through aerial silks wrapped around the performer. You see the constriction. You understand AND feel the restriction. Logic and intuition work together.

**Balance:** Not symbolic—demonstrated. Bodies in space showing actual equilibrium points. The Two of Pentacles isn't juggling drawn onto paper—it's a performer demonstrating dynamic balance on apparatus. You see the constant adjustment. You understand the concept AND recognize the lived experience of managing multiple forces.

**Suspension: **Not metaphorical—literal. Between earth and sky, action and rest, past and future. The Hanged Man isn't an illustration of someone hanging—it's a real body inverted on aerial web, actually experiencing the physical state of voluntary pause and shifted perspective. You see AND feel the state.

**Sacred geometry: **Not symbolic shapes overlaid on artwork—actual triangles, circles, spirals formed by apparatus and body in three-dimensional space. The Swords suit creates sharp, precise geometric patterns with aerial silks. You see the angles, the lines, the structures. The architecture of thought made physically visible.

The mystical revealed through the physical. The practical infused with sacred meaning. Integration at every level.

### **What This Means for Your Practice**

Images serve both understanding and intuition: Your analytical mind reads the physical structure, the geometry, the positioning. Your intuitive sense feels the energy, the state, the truth. Both work together—no choosing between logic and knowing.

Bodies demonstrate states you recognize: Not abstract symbols requiring external knowledge. Actual physical demonstrations of experiences you've lived. The constriction, the balance, the suspension—your body knows these states. Ancient wisdom grounded in embodied reality.

Physical positioning suggests application: When you see the actual state demonstrated, you know what it means to inhabit that position in your own life. The translation from card to practice becomes immediate. Spiritual insight with tangible traction.

Works whether you're new to tarot or deepening decades of existing practice. Visual and visceral truth speak to all levels of experience.
