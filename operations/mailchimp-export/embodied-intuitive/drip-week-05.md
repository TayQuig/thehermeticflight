## **Meeting the Court Cards**

*|FNAME|*, last week you mapped four territories.

This week, you meet the guides who live there.

Sixteen court cards. Sixteen personalities that seem to blur together—until you realize you already know them.

"Is the Knight of Cups the romantic one, or is that the Queen? 

What's the difference between a Page and a Knight? 

Why do some decks have Princes instead? 

How am I supposed to keep track of sixteen different people?"

Here's what nobody tells you:

You're not keeping track of strangers. 

You're meeting guides you'll recognize.

What if you already knew these people? 

What if they were characters you've encountered in every story you've ever loved? 

The curious student, 

he bold quester, 

the quiet sage, 

the commanding leader?

*What if meeting them in the cards was simply... meeting them again?*

Reflecting on my Reiki journey, I can plainly see where I’ve embodied the Page, the Knight, the Queen, and finally the King.

I embodied the Page when I was pursuing my training. Every day I woke up at 6am to study before my baby would need me. I would get my meditation, studying, and application in. 

I embodied the Knight when I took action and began treating new clients for free in order to gain experience applying the information I had studied so dutifully. 

I embodied the Knight when I created my Reiki business and started The Reiki Goddess. I didn’t wait until I was an expert. 

I answered the call.

As I grew through my practice and experience I became the Queen. I no longer doubted what I experienced during sessions. I understood when loved ones visited clients that they oftentimes would give me only one, peculiar word to share with my client. That single word has always meant something profound. 

Each and every time. 

I embodied the King when I decided to finally teach Reiki level One and Two. The outward expansion of my knowledge and experience. 

You can find these characters in your own life as well.

### **The Characters You'll Meet**

Imagine you're wandering through four different territories. The land of action and will. The realm of emotion and connection. The domain of thought and truth. 

The ground of material reality.

In each territory, you might meet four kinds of people:

**The Student (Pages)** — Someone just beginning to explore this territory. Curious. Open. Full of questions and wonder. They carry messages and possibilities. Everything is new to them, and that newness is their gift.

**The Quester (Knights)** — Someone actively pursuing something in this territory. In motion. Committed. Sometimes reckless, always driven. They're following their call wherever it leads, consequences be damned.

**The Sage Within (Queens)** — Someone who has mastered this territory internally. They know it in their bones, even if the world doesn't recognize it yet. Quiet authority. Deep, embodied wisdom. They don't need external validation.

**The Established Guide (Kings)** — Someone who has mastered this territory and now leads others through it. Their authority is recognized. Visible. Commanding. They've walked this path so thoroughly they can guide others.

So when you meet the Knight of Cups, you're encountering the Quester in emotional territory. Someone actively pursuing connection, romance, emotional experiences with everything they have. Heart-forward. Idealistic. 

Can you picture them?

When you meet the Queen of Cups, you're meeting the Sage Within that same emotional landscape. Someone who has mastered their own emotional world so deeply they can hold space for anyone's feelings. 

Can you feel the difference between these two, even in the same territory?

### **Why These Meetings Become Clearer**

### 

You're not memorizing sixteen separate, unrelated personalities.

You're meeting people at different stages of their journey through landscapes you're already exploring.

What makes the Knight of Cups different from the Queen of Cups? They're both in emotional territory, but one is actively questing while the other has found internal mastery. Same landscape, different relationship to it.

What makes the Knight of Cups different from the Knight of Swords? They're both questers, but one follows feeling while the other charges after truth. Same energy, different terrain.

Once you notice that you're meeting the same kinds of people (students, questers, sages, guides) in different landscapes (action, emotion, thought, material), the sixteen court cards stop being overwhelming.

They become... recognizable.

### **Meeting Them in Different Ways**

The beautiful thing? These guides can show up in your readings in different ways, and you get to discover which one feels right:

**As actual people:** Someone in your life who embodies this energy. "Oh, that's so-and-so. Total Knight of Wands energy; always chasing the next creative project."

**As parts of yourself:** An aspect of you showing up right now. "I'm being the Page of Pentacles in this situation; curious about new material possibilities, willing to learn."

**As invitations:** A suggestion for how to approach something. "What if you tried being the Queen of Swords here? What would that internal mental clarity look like?"

You're not locked into one interpretation. You're discovering which lens reveals the most.

### **Who Comes to Mind?**

### 

Think of someone you know well. Not to analyze them, just to wonder.

If they showed up as a court card, who would you meet?

Are they at the beginning of their journey in some territory (a Student)? 

Actively pursuing something (a Quester)? 

Quietly masterful (a Sage Within)? Recognized as a guide (an Established Guide)?

And which landscape do they most naturally inhabit? 

The territory of action and creativity? Emotion and connection? 

Thought and communication? Material reality and resources?

Who came to mind for you? 

I'm genuinely curious. Not because there's a right answer, but because I want to see what you notice when you start meeting these guides instead of memorizing them.

Send it. What do you discover about them? About yourself?
