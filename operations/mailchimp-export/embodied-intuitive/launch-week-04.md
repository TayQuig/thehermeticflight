## **Tarot Suits: 4 Territories to Explore**

## 

*|FNAME|*, for three weeks we've been talking about how you learn.

Now let's give you something to explore.

Four elements. Four suits. Four landscapes waiting for your *|TYPE_ADJ|* mind to wander through them.

This isn't memorization. 

This is cartography.

Four elements. Four suits. Fire, Water, Air, Earth.

They sound mystical until you realize they're territories. They’re four distinct landscapes, each with different terrain and weather and ways of moving through them.

What if you didn't need to memorize what they mean? What if you could simply... recognize where you are when you arrive?

We all know these energies simply because we have experienced life.

The fire of passion and will driving us to try new things and pursue things that bring us joy. 

I remember when I first experienced aerials.

My inner child came to life.

All of the passion and excitement of fire swelled up in my belly and the rest is history.

We all understand the familiar ebb and flow of water, emotion and intuition. 

That moment when we first fell in love. How easy it was to surrender to the flow of the warmth blooming inside of your chest. 

I remember when I first fell in love with my husband. I literally felt the “swept away” sensation of love coming in like high tide.

We all understand the earth energy of stability and consistency. Calm and steady. 

When we have a steady job or when all of our bills are paid.

We also know the opposite. Where we are struggling to make ends meet and need the food bank.

The energy of mental clarity and truth resonates with air. We know the energy of the cutting truth or when our thought process changes to quick and efficient. 

All of these energies are connected to a suit, or landscape, in the minor arcana, and we can pull from our precious life experiences to become familiar with them.

### **The Four Landscapes**

### 

**TORCHES / FIRE = The Territory of Will & Action**

This is where you go when you're asking about what you're doing, creating, building, initiating in the world. Creativity lives here. Passion. Career. Drive. Ambition. The spark of "what if I made this happen?" 

That excitement in your core.

When you pull a Torches card, you've arrived in action territory. The questions here sound like: 

What am I creating? 

Where's my passion moving? 

What wants to be born through me?

**CUPS / WATER = The Territory of Emotion & Connection**

This is where you go when you're exploring feelings, relationships, love, intuition, empathy. What happens inside you and between you and others. The landscape of the heart. The swelling inside your chest.

When you pull a Cups card, you're standing in emotional terrain. The questions shift: 

What am I feeling? 

How am I connecting? 

What does my heart know that my head doesn't?

**SWORDS / AIR = The Territory of Thought & Communication**

This is where you go when you're working with beliefs, conflicts, decisions, mental clarity, truth. The realm of what you think and how you speak it. Where ideas become words. The click in your brain when you connect the dots.

When you pull a Swords card, you've entered mental landscape. The questions become: 

What do I believe? 

What needs to be said? 

Where's the clarity, or the confusion?

**PENTACLES / EARTH = The Territory of Material & Physical**

This is where you go when you're dealing with money, health, home, resources, body. What you can touch. What sustains physical life. The ground beneath everything else. The familiar comfort of home.

When you pull a Pentacles card, you're on earthly terrain. The questions ground: 

What do I have? 

What do I need? 

How do I navigate material reality?

### **Why This Matters for Your Wandering**

Have you noticed that every situation in life already belongs somewhere?

A question about whether to quit your job lives primarily in action territory (Torches) or material territory (Pentacles). A question about a relationship lives in emotional territory (Cups). 

A question about what you believe lives in thought territory (Swords).

You're not trying to solve a mystical puzzle. You're just noticing which landscape your question already inhabits. You are already experiencing these landscapes daily.

And once you know the terrain? You can explore freely. Because each landscape has its own character, its own way of revealing what you need to see.

The NUMBER on the card suggests where you are in your journey through that landscape—at the beginning? In the middle challenge? At the completion point?

The SUIT tells you which territory you're wandering through.

What if that's all you needed to begin understanding any card?

### **Curious Observations Across Territories**

Here's something you might notice as you wander:

All the ACES feel like beginnings, regardless of which territory you find them in. The Ace of Wands begins something in action. 

The Ace of Cups begins something in emotion. 

The Ace of Pentacles begins something material. Same energy of "yes, here's the seed", different ground it's planted in.

All the FIVES feel like challenge points. 

Disruption. 

The moment things get difficult or chaotic. 

But a Five in Wands territory (conflict around action/creativity) feels different from a Five in Cups territory (emotional loss), even though both carry that "crisis" energy.

All the TENS feel like completion or culmination. 

The full expression of that territory's journey. 

But completion in Torches (maybe burnout from over-doing) looks different from completion in Cups (emotional fulfillment or family legacy).

Same stage of the path. Different landscapes.

What if you could explore ten stages through four contexts, and that gave you access to all 78 cards?

### **An Expanding Exercise**

Think of a situation you're actually navigating right now. Something real in your life.

Which landscape does it primarily belong in?

Is it about what you're doing or creating? (Action territory) 

Is it about what you're feeling or how you're relating? (Emotion territory) 

Is it about what you're thinking or needing to communicate? (Thought territory) 

Is it about physical resources or material concerns? (Material territory)

You're not looking for the "right" answer. 

You're just noticing: where does this question naturally live?

If you have cards, try pulling three from that suit. What do they want to show you about navigating this specific terrain?

If you don't have cards yet, just wonder: what would you want to know about moving through this landscape? 

What questions would guide your exploration?

### **An Invitation**

Pick one suit that calls to you right now. The territory that feels most alive or most relevant or just most interesting.

If you have a deck, pull three cards from that suit. Notice what shifts as you move through the same landscape. 

Do you see progression? 

Patterns? 

What does the terrain reveal?

If you don't have cards, sit with one territory for a few minutes. 

What draws you to it? 

What do you imagine you might discover there?

Let me know what you found. What does the landscape show you? What are you curious about? I want to hear which territory called to you and why.
