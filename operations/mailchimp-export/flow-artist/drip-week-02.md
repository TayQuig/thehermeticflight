## **When Flow Becomes Flood**

*|FNAME|*, last week I told you that your *|TYPE_ADJ|* mind isn't broken.

That's true.

But it's not the whole truth.

Every gift casts a shadow. And if I'm going to earn your trust over these weeks, I need to show you I see both sides.

Let me tell you about the time my own creative process nearly drowned me.

During the 2020 Pandemic, I had so much time on my hands.

Who didn't?

I discovered Reiki and began my training soon after the shutdowns began.

I fell in love with all of itвАФthe meditations, the symbols, the stories about Usui and how he discovered this beautiful magic.

Reiki became a way of living for me and my family. It wove itself into everything.

It still does.

After a couple of years of practice, a few people began asking me to teach them.

Even after two full years with over 100 clients, I felt like I had no business sharing what I knew. It had *only* been two years.

Despite wanting to teach, I felt like I didn't know enough.

I sought mentors. Became a Reiki master. Continued my research. Explored other styles.

For years, I let the shadow of my curiosity hold me back from the natural unfolding of my journey.

I feared stagnation.

My nervous system felt it.

The truth is, curiosity is a gift.

Until it becomes a shield.

There's a shadow that lives in the seeker's heart, and we need to talk about it honestly: the fear that if you claim what you know, you'll stop being the seeker. That if you land somewhere, you lose permission to keep exploring.

Allow me to show you both sides.

### **The Shadow of Infinite Possibility**

You know this feeling. Starting something newвАФa project, a practice, a creative explorationвАФwith so much excitement. The energy is electric. Everything feels possible.

And then... another new thing calls. And another. And another.

Nothing ever gets finished because something more interesting always emerges. You're collecting beautiful beginnings, but the middles and endings never come.

This is the shadow: chaos masquerading as creativity.

Using "I'm just following my intuition" as permission to avoid the harder work of committing. Of going deep instead of wide. Of finishing something even when the initial spark has faded and you're left with just discipline and devotion.

### **The Structure Paradox**

Here's what sounds like a contradiction but isn't:

Flow needs riverbanks or it becomes flood.

Water without containment doesn't carve canyons or nourish fields. It just spreads everywhere and evaporates.

Some structure isn't a cage. It's a container that holds your creativity so it can actually go somewhere.

The artists you admireвАФthe ones creating work that moves youвАФthey all have practice. Ritual. Consistency. They show up even when they don't feel like it. They finish things.

That's not betraying intuition. That's honoring it enough to give it form.

### **Where Your Gift Becomes a Trap**

You can dismiss all thinking as "too much in my head." But sometimes thinking helps. Analysis serves creation.

You can resist anything that feels like "rules"вАФbut guidance isn't imprisonment. Teachers aren't trying to kill your creativity. Structure doesn't have to mean rigidity.

You can create so much beauty that never gets shared because you're afraid committing to something means closing off other possibilities.

But here's the thing: you can hold both. Flow AND form. Intuition AND structure. Freedom AND discipline.

They're not opposites. They're dance partners.

### **The Question**

No reply needed this week. No assignment. Just sit with this:

Where might you be using "intuition" or "creative flow" to avoid something harder?

Not to shame yourself. Just to see it clearly.

Because once you see it, you can choose something different.
