## **Depth Is Your Tarot Superpower**

*|FNAME|*, we've talked about your gifts. We've talked about your shadows.

Now let's talk about what your *|TYPE_ADJ|* mind can actually do with a deck of cards.

Because here's what most tarot teachers won't tell you: the way you already see the world? It's exactly how the cards want to be read.

Here's what nobody tells seekers:

Your questions *are* the practice. Not preparation for practice. Not what you do until you know enough. The questions themselvesвҖ”the wondering, the what-if, the turning cards over in your mindвҖ”that IS the work.

I remember when I first started training aerial under a coach who was an incredible performer but, weirdly enough, hated teaching.

I was so excited about learning that I forgot to ask questions.

I had no idea certain moves weren't supposed to hurt.

I accidentally trained aerial silk four days a week on partially subluxed shoulders for six entire weeks.

All because I didn't ask questions.

My shoulders kept slipping out of socket, but I loved aerial so much that I kept trainingвҖ”icing like a mad woman, pushing through the pain.

I visited my insanely patient chiropractor twice a week. He would say something like, "You should really consider stopping aerial" or "Take a break."

But my heart couldn't let go.

It wasn't until a beautiful PT who specializes in aerialists came into my life that I finally understood.

I wasn't fully engaging my shoulders and upper back. My shoulders were just hanging. I thought it was proper form because that's how I'd been taught.

I didn't ask my coach questions, and she wasn't offering any body mechanics guidance.

I worked with my PT for months and discovered that engaging my shoulders and upper back while hanging kept them in their sockets.

It was life-changing.

My chiropractor was happy. My shoulders began to heal and strengthen. My form rapidly improved.

Best of all? No more falling asleep with ice packs every night.

Those lessons wove more curiosity into me, and I discovered that constantly seeking not only kept my body safeвҖ”it made me a better aerialist and instructor.

And how does this flow into tarot?

It's all about embracing that curiosity.

### **Your Creative Lens Is a Tarot Superpower**

You don't see the cards as fixed symbols with predetermined meanings. You see them as living art.

You let cards speak to each other the way colors do on a paletteвҖ”this one intensifies that one, these three create tension, those two create harmony.

You trust aesthetic resonance. "These three cards feel right together" is valid information to you, even if you can't articulate why yet.

And that fluidityвҖ”that willingness to let meaning emerge rather than forcing itвҖ”reveals nuances that rigid interpretation misses.

### **How Flow Artists Read Differently**

You start with what draws your eye, not what the book says you should notice.

Maybe it's the color of someone's cloak. Maybe it's the way a figure's hands are positioned. Maybe it's the empty space in the corner of the image that suddenly feels significant.

You let the reading unfold. You don't march through positions one-two-three checking off meanings. You feel where the energy wants to go, which cards are talking to each other, what wants to be known first.

You trust the pauses. The white space. The card you almost pulled but didn't. The one that fell out of the deck while shuffling.

readings have rhythm. Pacing. They breathe.

### **What This Brings to Tarot Practice**

Freshness. The cards never become stale when you approach them as creative collaboration instead of rote memorization.

Depth. Beauty reveals layers. When you're really looking at the art, really feeling into the imagery, you see things you'd never notice if you were just checking a keyword list.

Accessibility. You make tarot feel approachable, not intimidating. People relax around your readings because they sense your openness.

Innovation. You find spread designs, card combinations, and insights others never imagine because you're not locked into "this is how it's done."

### **Try This**

Pull three cards right now. Any three.

Don't look up meanings. Don't consult the guidebook.

Just notice:

What colors dominate? What feeling emerges when you look at all three together? If these were three paintings in a gallery, what story would they tell?

Hit reply with what you discover. I'm genuinely curious what your creative eye sees.
