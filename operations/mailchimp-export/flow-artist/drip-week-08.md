## **The Vocabulary of Transformation**

*|FNAME|*, last week I told you the cards are meant to be seen, not just memorized.

But what if they could also be *felt*?

Most tarot decks show frozen moments. Static poses. The figure in the card isn't movingвҖ”they're held in amber.

What would change if they could breathe?

Body language is something every human understands.

Think of dancers. We can see their emotion in how they move.

Your body understands this unspoken language because we all share the same hardwareвҖ”the nervous system.

I'm reminded of one of my dear friends and her performance journey.

When she first started performing regularly, you could see in her body how timid she really was.

No eye contact with the audience. Shoulders slightly rolled forward. Moving much faster in performance than in practice.

This slowly evolved over time. Chest open. Eyes locking with audience members. Perfectly rhythmic, intentional movements.

She went from beginner to professional after years of practice and repetition.

It has been an honor to watch.

The audience can feel when someone is afraid of them versus when someone is electrified by their attention.

And this translates directly to our cards.

The art in our cards are still images. Frozen moments. And yet, the meaning moves.

It flows through gesture. Radiates from posture. Pulses in the space between figures.

What if the bodyвҖ”yours and the bodies in the cardsвҖ”already knows what the mind is still trying to figure out?

### **Movement as Language**

Bodies in space communicate what static images can only suggest.

Watch someone walk into a room. Before they say a word, you knowвҖ”are they confident or uncertain? Open or defended? Present or distracted?

Posture reveals psychological state without language.

The way someone holds themselves tells the whole story.

### **What Movement Makes Visible**

Physical tension mirrors emotional tension. You see it in shoulders drawn up, in hands clenched, in a body bracing for impact.

Balance shows the constant adjustment between opposing forces. Watch someone on one footвҖ”they're never perfectly still. They're making a thousand tiny corrections, negotiating gravity and intention.

Suspension is the pause. Between action and rest. Between earth and sky. Between what was and what's coming next. That held moment where everything is potential.

Direction shows where energy wants to go. Ascending. Descending. Opening. Closing. Reaching. Withdrawing.

All of this is happening in bodies, in space, in time.

### **Why This Resonates for Creative Minds**

You understand metaphor through the body.

When someone says "I feel stuck," you see it. In their posture. In how they move through space. In the way they hold themselves.

When something is "unbalanced" in your life, you feel it physically. Like trying to walk on one foot with your arms full.

Dance, gesture, movement, physical artвҖ”these all speak a language you're already fluent in.

Embodied wisdom is your native tongue.

### **What This Means for Tarot**

Imagine the Eight of SwordsвҖ”not drawn, but lived.

Real silks wrapped around a real body. You see the precise angle of the binding. The exact degree of constriction. The tension between wanting to break free and not quite knowing how.

Imagine the Two of PentaclesвҖ”not juggling illustrated spheres, but an actual performer demonstrating dynamic balance. You watch the constant adjustment. The lived experience of managing multiple forces.

The Hanged Man, actually inverted. The physical sensation of blood rushing to the head, of seeing the world differently because your body is oriented differently.

Body positioning shows card meaning.

Not suggested. Demonstrated.
