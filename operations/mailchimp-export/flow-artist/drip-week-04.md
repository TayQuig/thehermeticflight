## **Four Ways Energy Wants to Move**

*|FNAME|*, for three weeks we've been talking about you.

Now let's talk about the cards.

But not the way most people teach them—not memorization, not keyword lists, not "The Three of Cups means celebration."

Let's talk about the architecture underneath. The patterns your *|TYPE_ADJ|* eye has probably already started to notice.

Four elements. Four suits. Fire, Water, Air, Earth.

They sound mystical until you realize they're territories. Four distinct landscapes, each with different terrain and weather and ways of moving through them.

What if you didn't need to memorize what they mean? What if you could simply... feel where you are when you arrive?

We already know these energies. We've lived them.

The fire of passion and will that moves us to try new things—to chase what brings us joy.

I remember when I first experienced aerials. My inner child came alive. All that fire swelled up in my belly, and the rest is history.

We understand the ebb and flow of water—emotion and intuition.

That moment when we first fell in love. How easy it was to surrender to the warmth blooming inside your chest.

I remember falling in love with my husband. I literally felt swept away—love rushing in like high tide.

We understand earth energy—stability and steadiness. Calm and grounded.

When we have a steady job. When our bills are paid.

We also know the opposite: struggling to make ends meet, needing the food bank.

Air carries the energy of mental clarity and truth. We know the cutting edge of truth, or when our thoughts become quick and clear.

All of these energies flow through a suit in the minor arcana, and we can pull meaning from our own precious life experiences to feel our way through them.

### **The Four Creative Territories**

Let me show you what I mean.

**WANDS / FIRE = Creative Action & Drive**

This is passion projects. New beginnings. The 3am burst of "what if I made this?" energy that gets you out of bed.

This is the rhythm of initiation. The spark. The moment something that didn't exist suddenly wants to exist, and you're the one who has to bring it through.

Wands = the energy that starts things.

**CUPS / WATER = Emotional Flow & Connection**

This is how you feel. How you relate. What moves through your heart and spills into your art.

This is the rhythm of receiving and releasing. The tides. Creating from emotional overflow—whether that's joy, grief, longing, or love.

Cups = the energy of depth, the well you draw from.

**SWORDS / AIR = Mental Clarity & Expression**

This is ideas taking shape. The vague feeling finally becoming clear concept. Communication. The sharp edge of truth cutting through confusion.

This is the rhythm of articulation. Of finally being able to say what you mean. Of thought becoming form.

Swords = the energy that gives shape to what was shapeless.

**PENTACLES / EARTH = Material Creation & Manifestation**

This is what you build. What you can touch. The physical object that results from all that inspiration and feeling and thinking.

This is the rhythm of completion. Of landing. Resources, body, the material world.

Pentacles = the energy that brings things into form you can hold.

### **How These Rhythms Show Up in Your Creative Life**

You already know these rhythms. You just might not have named them.

Wands is that bolt of inspiration that won't leave you alone until you act on it.

Cups is creating from the place of feeling—needing to express what's moving through you emotionally.

Swords is the moment clarity strikes and suddenly you know exactly what you're trying to say.

Pentacles is finally finishing the thing. Sharing it. Making it real in the world.

Same creative journey. Different mediums.

### **Creative Energy in Motion**

Here's what makes this elegant:

You're not memorizing forty random cards. You're learning ten stages in four different rhythms.

Every Ace is a beginning—but Ace of Wands is a creative spark, Ace of Cups is an emotional opening, Ace of Swords is a mental breakthrough, Ace of Pentacles is a material opportunity.

Same concept (seed, potential, start). Different medium.

Every Five is tension, crisis, breakthrough. Five of Wands = creative conflict. Five of Cups = emotional loss. Five of Swords = mental defeat. Five of Pentacles = material struggle.

Same stage of the journey. Different territory.

Every Ten is completion. But what that completion looks like depends on the suit—creative culmination, emotional fulfillment, mental resolution, material legacy.

### **The Rhythm Across All Four**

All Aces whisper: beginning. Spark, seed, potential, the "yes" at the start.

All Fives sing: conflict. Chaos, disruption, the crisis point where breakthrough lives.

All Tens declare: completion. Culmination, ending, the full expression of that element's energy.

Numbers whisper where you are in the creative cycle. Suits sing which element is moving.

Once you feel the rhythm, every card makes sense.
