## **Four Ways We Avoid Truth**

*|FNAME|*, most tarot teachers start with the light.

Fire for passion. Water for love. Air for clarity. Earth for abundance.

That's one way to see it.

But you already know: every element has a shadow side. And until you understand both, you're only reading half the card.

Let me show you what's underneath the surface meanings.

Four elements. Four suits. Fire, Water, Air, Earth.

They're not mystical concepts. They're territories you've already walked through—whether you had names for them or not.

You don't need to memorize what they mean. You've already lived them. The question is whether you're willing to recognize what you've experienced.

### **Fire: Will and Hunger**

We all know fire. That consuming drive that won't let you rest until you pursue what you want. Not polite passion—raw hunger.

When I first experienced aerials, something primal woke up. Not my "inner child." Something fiercer. A hunger I'd been politely suppressing finally given permission to burn.

That's fire. The part of you that refuses to stay small.

**Water: Surrender and Drowning**

Water is the ebb and flow of emotion—but let's be honest about what that actually means. The moment you fall in love and realize you've lost control. The "swept away" feeling that's half-ecstasy, half-terror.

When I fell for my husband, I felt love come in like high tide. Beautiful, yes. But also: I was drowning in something bigger than my ability to manage it.

That's water. The vulnerability of letting something move through you without resistance.

### **Earth: Security and Collapse**

Earth is stability. Bills paid. Steady job. Ground under your feet.

Until it isn't.

We also know what it feels like when the ground gives out. When you're at the food bank wondering how you'll make rent. When "stability" reveals itself as the temporary illusion it always was.

That's earth. The material reality that both holds you and can crumble without warning.

### **Air: Truth That Cuts**

Air is mental clarity. The moment your thinking sharpens and you suddenly see what's actually happening. Not comfortable truth—the kind that slices through every story you've been telling yourself.

The thought that arrives and changes everything because now you can't un-know it.

That's air. The cutting edge of honest perception.

### **These Suits Are Your Life**

Every card in the minor arcana lives in one of these territories. You don't need tarot books to understand fire, water, air, and earth. You need to be willing to acknowledge what you've already experienced.

The comfortable parts and the parts you'd rather forget.

That's how you learn the suits—not through memorization, but through recognition.

By facing what you already know.

### **The Four Domains of Experience**

These aren't mystical. They're the territories of human life.

**TORCHES / FIRE = Will & Action** What you do in the world. Creativity, passion, career, drive, ambition, the projects you build and the actions you take. This is the domain of doing, making, initiating.

**CUPS / WATER = Emotion & Connection** What you feel and how you relate. Relationships, emotions, intuition, love, empathy, the internal landscape and the bridges between people. This is the domain of feeling, connecting, relating.

**SWORDS / AIR = Thought & Communication** What you think and how you speak. Beliefs, conflicts, decisions, mental clarity, truth-telling, the realm of ideas and words. This is the domain of mind, logic, communication.

**PENTACLES / EARTH = Material & Physical** What you have and how you survive. Money, health, home, resources, body, the tangible world that sustains physical life. This is the domain of material reality, earthly concerns.

Every situation in life falls primarily into one of these domains. Not arbitrary—actual categories of human experience.

### **Truth and Avoidance in Each Domain**

Here's what nobody tells you: every domain contains both the truth of that realm AND the ways we hide from it.

**TORCHES:** Taking action when it's time vs. endless planning that never becomes action. Vision without execution. Burnout from doing without meaning—or meaning without doing. The Ace of Torches is the spark of creative force. The Ten of Torches is the burden of carrying what you started. Between them: the ways we avoid actually doing the thing.

**CUPS:** Feeling deeply and honestly vs. numbing, dissociating, drowning in others' feelings to avoid facing your own. Emotional codependence dressed up as empathy. The Ace of Cups is emotional opening. The Five of Cups is genuine loss. Between them: the ways we avoid feeling what's true.

**SWORDS:** Clear thinking and honest communication vs. intellectualizing to avoid feeling. Analysis as armor. Mental gymnastics that justify lies we're telling ourselves. Thinking your way around truth instead of through it. The Ace of Swords is breakthrough clarity. The Eight of Swords is the binding of false beliefs. Between them: the ways we avoid thinking clearly.

**PENTACLES:** Facing material reality vs. hoarding, scarcity mentality, worshipping security until it becomes a prison. Confusing stability with stagnation. Pretending resources, bodies, and survival don't matter. The Ace of Pentacles is material beginning. The Five of Pentacles is actual lack. Between them: the ways we avoid looking at what we actually have or don't have.

The "negative" cards aren't warnings. They're revelations showing where the avoidance lives.

### **Why This Matters for Depth Work**

You can't transform what you won't acknowledge.

Each suit shows where shadow lives in that domain. The cards don't let you avoid—they put the avoidance right there in the spread.

When the Eight of Swords appears, it's showing you: here's where your thinking is binding you. Not philosophically—actually. What false belief are you treating as truth?

When the Five of Cups shows up, it's revealing: here's where you're pretending loss isn't real. Not metaphorically—actually. What are you avoiding grieving?

The Tower in Torches: here's what you've built that needs to fall. The Tower in Cups: here's what emotional structure is collapsing. The Tower in Swords: here's what belief system is shattering. The Tower in Pentacles: here's what material security is crumbling.

Same card energy (necessary destruction), different domain (what specifically is falling).

Tarot shows you where you're hiding in each realm of life. That's the structure. That's why the suits matter.
