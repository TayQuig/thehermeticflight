## **A Deck That Doesn't Flinch**

*|FNAME|*,¬†

I hit a ceiling in aerial that I couldn't break through. And for months, I convinced myself it was just my body's limitation.

I'd learned a set of moves on silks. Mastered them. But watching other women my age execute far more difficult progressions, I knew something was wrong.

I just couldn't see what.

So I did what people do when they're avoiding an uncomfortable truth: I told myself we just had different bodies. Different capacities. That comparing myself was the problem, not my actual practice.

That was easier than admitting I was missing something fundamental.

### **What I Was Refusing to See**

It wasn't until I left my studio and took classes somewhere new that the truth became unavoidable:

I was literally missing foundational information. Information that other studios systematically teach. Information I should have had from day one.

Remember the shoulder issue I mentioned before? How I'd been training on partially subluxed shoulders because I didn't know how to engage them properly?

This was bigger than that.

I'd been told to "condition, condition, condition" and "stretch all the time." But nobody had taught me *how* to condition for aerial specifically. How to stretch in ways that actually supported the unique mechanical demands of hanging, inverting, and suspending your entire body weight from fabric or steel.

I had a decade of gymnastics. I'd run fitness groups. I had yoga training.

None of it prepared me for aerial's specific requirements. And I'd been too proudвАФor too afraidвАФto admit I didn't know what I was doing.

### **The Revelation Wasn't Comfortable**

When the new studio showed me what I'd been missing, it wasn't some beautiful unlock moment.

It was confronting how much time I'd wasted working *around* my ignorance instead of addressing it.

Suddenly, the mysterious progression I'd been trying to decode wasn't mysterious at all. It was systematic. There was a roadmapвАФI just hadn't been given it. Or hadn't been willing to ask for it.

The disconnected pieces of information I'd been collecting finally integrated. My practice accelerated.

New pathways on silk appeared. Different combinations in lyra became possible.

And I could finally decode what I was watching in accomplished aerialists' performances. The mystery dissolved because I could see the underlying structure I'd been missing all along.
