## **The Shadow of Going Deep**

## 

*|FNAME|*, last week I told you that your depth-seeking nature is a gift.

That's true.

Here's the part most people won't say out loud: every gift has a cost.

Your willingness to descend into difficult territory can become its own trap. And if I'm going to earn your trust, I need to show you I understand where that edge lives.

Let me tell you about the time I mistook darkness for wisdom.

The 2020 pandemic gave me something most people spent their whole lives avoiding: uninterrupted time to face what I'd been running from.

I discovered Reiki during the shutdowns. Dove into the meditations, the symbols, the story of Usui's discovery. The practice didn't just transform my daysвҖ”it rewired how my family moved through life. Still does.

Two years in, after more than 100 clients, people started asking me to teach them.

My answer? No.

Not because I didn't want to. Because I'd convinced myself I hadn't earned it. Two years wasn't enough. I needed more training. More mentors. More research. I studied other Reiki lineages. Pursued my master certification. Read everything I could find.

For years, I let curiosity become my cage.

Here's the truth nobody wants to say out loud: curiosity is a gift until you weaponize it against yourself.

There's a shadow that lives in the perpetual seeker's heart. The terror that if you claim what you knowвҖ”if you actually land somewhere and say "I know this"вҖ”you'll lose permission to keep exploring. That arrival means stagnation. That teaching means you stop being the student.

So you stay searching. Forever.

Not because there's more to learn. Because learning is safer than doing.

My nervous system knew something was wrong long before my mind admitted it. Restlessness. That familiar ache of denying what wants to emerge.

I was hiding behind "not ready yet."

The real fear? That if I stepped into teaching, I'd be exposed as someone who didn't have all the answers. As if that's not exactly what makes a good teacherвҖ”someone still willing to face uncertainty.

Curiosity kept me safe. And small.

Let me show you what lives on both sides of that edge.

### **The Shadow Dancer's Shadow**

Every archetype has one. Yours is this:

You can glorify struggleвҖ”making pain a prerequisite for wisdom, as if suffering is the only teacher that matters.

You can make darkness your identityвҖ”if you're not wrestling with something heavy, if you're not "doing the work," you're being shallow. Joy becomes suspect. Lightness becomes weakness.

You can spiritually bypass through shadow workвҖ”using your willingness to "go deep" as a way to avoid genuine connection with people who just want to be close to you without excavating their childhood trauma first.

The trap: depth becomes performance, not process. You're not actually transformingвҖ”you're proving you're deep.

### **How This Shows Up**

You dismiss anything light as "toxic positivity" when sometimes it's just... joy. Someone's genuinely happy and you need to point out what they're not seeing. You can't let simple things be simple.

You're always the one who "goes there"вҖ”even when the moment doesn't need depth, even when it's actually appropriate to stay on the surface.

You make everything profound when sometimes things are just what they are. Not every conversation needs to become therapy. Not every experience needs to be mined for meaning.

You use depth to create distance from people who want to get close. They have to prove they're "deep enough" to be in your life. You've made shadow work a wall instead of a bridge.

### **The Way Through**

Depth is a gift when it serves truth. It becomes a trap when it serves your identity.

Real transformation includes emergence, not just descent. You can't live in the underworld and call it enlightenment. At some point, you have to come back up with what you've learned.

Sometimes the bravest thing is letting something be light. Not everything requires analysis. Not every moment needs to be transformed into a lesson.

You don't have to prove your depth by living in perpetual darkness.

The shadow you need to see: you might be using your willingness to face hard things as a way to avoid facing simple things. Like joy. Like ease. Like someone who just wants to laugh with you without discussing the void first.

**This Is the Hard One**

This email is harder to receive than last week's validation.

But if I'm going to be honest with youвҖ”and you need honesty more than comfortвҖ”then I have to name this.

Your gift becomes your cage when depth becomes your whole identity. When you can't experience lightness without suspicion. When you use shadow work to avoid genuine connection.

You can hold both. Depth and lightness. Transformation and joy. The underworld and the surface.

That's actual integration.
