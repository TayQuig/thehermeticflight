** Permission to Begin Without Knowing.
------------------------------------------------------------

*|FNAME|*, you came through as The *|ARCHETYPE|*.

Think back to those quiz questions—the ones where you chose exploration over certainty, where "I wonder" felt more true than "I know."

Those answers came easily, didn't they?

Not because you were guessing. Because you were recognizing yourself.

Let me tell you about the time I learned this the hard way.

When I was working through my Reiki level Two training, my reiki Master, Zachariah Grace, assigned actual treatments for a large portion of our weekly lessons.

Every week following the completion of Reiki Level One, the memorization symbols, and attunements, we were supposed to find people who were interested in receiving Reiki in order to gain experience.

I had only ever done Reiki on myself at that point.

I had no idea what it would be like giving a complete stranger Reiki.

It was nothing short of terrifying.

Curiosity tortured my nervous system.

I couldn’t ask my Reiki master enough questions to quell the wondering in my heart.

It was only after I experienced my first client, that everything changed for me.

Once I embodied the role, took action, and DID what was organic to the human experience, did I fully understand that; actually channeling Reiki energy for a client was invaluable to my learning.

The actual doing taught me more than any conversations, lessons, or sacred texts ever could.

I felt my energy centers widen and warm with the increased flow of energy running through my physical body.

I received information from my client’s energy body that I can only imagine is possible through the attunements and Reiki opening my chakras wider.

I had become receptive and a channel.

I discovered intuitive gifts I never knew I had.

I knew when to use each symbol. When to move on from a certain area of the body. When to actually lay my hands on them and when to just hover gently over the spot asking for energy...

And I didn’t need instructions.

I needed to be brave enough to embark on the adventure — to step into the unknown.

And IF I had not been open to receiving, brave enough to jump in and DO… We wouldn’t be here together.

So, what if you don't need all the answers before you start?

What if curiosity, not certainty, is exactly what tarot asks of you?

The cards don't require you to arrive as an expert. They invite you to explore.


** The Sign You've Been Waiting For
------------------------------------------------------------


**
------------------------------------------------------------

Here's what nobody tells people drawn to tarot: the practice rewards the curious mind, not the certain one.

"Not knowing" isn't a gap you need to fix before you can begin. It's the space where discovery actually happens. Your questions, the genuine, wondering, "I'm not sure but maybe..." questions, those are more valuable than any answer you could borrow from someone else's guidebook.

Whether you pulled your first card yesterday or you've been reading for decades, approaching with wonder keeps the practice alive. The moment you think you know everything is the moment the cards stop teaching you.

Your willingness to not-know? That's your access point.


** Why Your Approach Works
------------------------------------------------------------


**
------------------------------------------------------------

Here's what happens: while others need complete systems and total understanding before they feel safe beginning, you learn by doing.

By exploring.

By trying things and seeing what happens. Embodying fire energy and taking action.

And that openness, that willingness to sit with uncertainty, gives you access to meanings others miss. Tarot speaks most clearly to minds that stay open. And yours does.

Exploration isn't a phase you graduate from. It's not "beginner energy" you're supposed to evolve past. Deep practitioners, people who've worked with tarot for thirty years, will tell you the same thing: the wondering never stops.

It just goes deeper.

When you stay curious, you stay connected, and energy flows. When you think you've figured it out, you've closed the door.


** What This Means for Your Practice
------------------------------------------------------------


**
------------------------------------------------------------

You don't need to memorize 78 cards before you begin.

You don't need to know the "right" interpretation.

You don't need permission from anyone to pull a card and wonder what it means.

Your "I wonder what this means..." IS the start of interpretation. It's not a failure to know, it's the beginning of discovery.

Trust the pull toward cards that intrigue you.

Follow the thread of your curiosity.

Ask questions that don't have obvious answers.

Let yourself explore without destination.

Your path might look meandering to people who need straight lines and clear endpoints. That's because you're covering more territory. You're seeing things they rush past.

That's not a problem to fix.

That's your gift in action.
