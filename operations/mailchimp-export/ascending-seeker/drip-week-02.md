## **The Gift and the Trap of Eternal Seeking**

*|FNAME|*, last week I told you that your *|TYPE_ADJ|* mind isn't broken.

That's true.

But it's not the whole truth.

Every gift casts a shadow. And if I'm going to earn your trust over these weeks, I need to show you I see both sides.

Let me tell you about the years I held myself back.

During the 2020 Pandemic, I had so much time on my hands.

Who didnвҖҷt?

I discovered Reiki and began my training soon after the shut downs began.

I fell in love with all of the meditations, the symbols, the stories about Usui and how he discovered the magic that is Reiki.В 

I embodied the practice so well that Reiki really became a way of living for me and my family.

It still is to this day.

After a couple of years of practice I had a few individuals asking me to teach them.

Even after I had practiced for two entire years with well over 100 clients, I felt like I had no business sharing what I knew because it had only been two years.

In spite of the fact that yes, I did want to teach, I felt as though I did not know enough.

I sought mentors, became a reiki master, continued my own research, even looked into other invented Reiki styles.

For literal years, I allowed the shadow of my curiosity to hold me back from the natural expansion of my personal journey.

I feared stagnation.В 

My nervous system knew it.

The truth is, curiosity is a gift.

Until it becomes a shield.

There's a shadow that lives in the seekerвҖҷs heart, and we need to talk about it honestly: the fear that if you claim what you know, you'll stop being the seeker. That if you land somewhere, you'll lose the permission to keep searching.

Allow me to show you both sides.

### **The Gift: Beginner's Mind at Any Stage**

You see what others miss because you haven't decided what things "should" mean. Your openness to multiple interpretations creates readings with depth and nuance that rigid certainty can't access.

It makes you unique.

You stay teachable when others get defensive about their methods. The world genuinely needs tarot readers who approach each spread with fresh eyesвҖ”even after thousands of readings.

Professional readers with decades of experience will tell you: maintaining curiosity is the advanced practice. The ability to still wonder, still discover, still let cards surprise you? 

That's mastery, not naivety.

Your exploratory nature isn't something you'll grow out of. It's something others wish they could return to.

### **The Shadow: Seeking as Avoidance**

### 

But here's the uncomfortable truth:

"I'm still learning" can become a way to never claim your knowing. A permanent excuse to avoid being seen, to dodge vulnerability, to deflect responsibility for what you actually see. Avoid stepping fully into your power and expansion.В 

Collecting information without ever integrating it. Asking the same questions repeatedly to avoid acting on the answers you've already received. 

Staying in exploration mode to dodge the exposure of saying "this is what I see."

The freedom to wonder becomes a cage when it prevents you from landing.

Somewhere along the way, curiosity stops being about genuine discovery and starts being about staying safe. If you're always seeking, you never have to claim. If you never claim, you never have to be wrong. If you never risk being wrong, you never have to be vulnerable.

And that, that's when your gift becomes your trap.

We canвҖҷt evolve.

### **The Integration: Both/And**

Here's what most people won't tell you because they're afraid it sounds contradictory:

You can claim expertise AND maintain curiosity. You can say "I know this" and still discover more. These aren't opposites, they're partners.

Mastery doesn't close the door to wonder. It opens *deeper* ones. The more you understand, the more complex and beautiful the questions become.

"I know this, and I'm still discovering more", that's the practitioner's mantra. Not "I'm still learning so I can't claim anything." Not "I know everything so I'm done exploring."

Both. Always both.

Your gift isn't just exploration. Your gift is bringing back what you find and saying "look." Having the courage to say "here's what I see" even when you know there's more you haven't seen yet. Your perspective is unique and it's needed.

That's where the real practice lives. In the courage to land while staying open to flight.

### **The Honest Question**

What knowledge about tarot, or about yourself, have you been avoiding claiming?

What do you actually know that you're still calling "just my interpretation" or "I'm not sure but..."?

What would it cost you to say "yes, I see this clearly"?

Send your best intention. This is a safe space. I'm asking because naming it is the first step toward owning it.

And owning it doesn't mean stopping exploration. It means having somewhere to explore from.
