# Research: Email Gate Placement Best Practices

## Key Findings

### Optimal Placement: After Last Question, Before Results

The universal best practice across all major quiz platforms (Interact, Typeform, Outgrow, Thrive Themes) is identical: **place the email capture after the final question but before revealing results**.

- "Always ask for contact details after users have invested time answering questions but before revealing their results. This strategic placement creates a 'micro-commitment' effect."
  - Source: https://outgrow.co/blog/quiz-engagement-benchmarks-completion-rates
- "Ask for the email after the last question, before showing results, and explain exactly what they'll get. By then, curiosity is high."
  - Source: https://emailconversionlab.com/2025/09/03/how-to-build-a-high-converting-quiz-to-email-funnel/

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks

| Metric | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Quiz start-to-lead (average) | 40.1% | Interact 2026 Report |
| Email opt-in when gate placed before results | 30-40% | Multiple sources |
| Form completion among those who see the gate | 47-50% | Riddle Lab study |
| Completion of opt-in form at end (before results) | 92-97% | Thrive Themes, Dupple |
| Standard lead magnet (PDF download) | ~5% | Dupple comparison |

**Critical stat**: 97% of people who complete a quiz submit the opt-in form when it appears between last question and results reveal.
- Source: https://thewellpaidexpert.com/quiz-funnels/

### Why This Works (Psychology)

1. **Sunk cost / micro-commitment**: After 7-10 questions, users have invested 2+ minutes. The email is a small additional ask.
2. **Curiosity gap**: Users have answered questions about themselves and are psychologically primed to see the result. The email field is a minor friction barrier against a strong curiosity pull.
3. **Perceived value exchange**: "Enter your email to see your personalized result" frames email as a fair trade, not a cold ask.

### Implementation Best Practices

- Make the email field the ONLY thing on the gate screen -- no distractions
- Copy should be minimal and value-focused: "Enter your email to see your archetype result"
- Include a privacy/trust signal ("We'll never spam you")
- Optional: offer additional value ("...and receive your personalized archetype guide")
- Skip option: Some quizzes allow "skip" but show degraded results -- NOT recommended for lead gen
- Source: https://thrivethemes.com/how-to-create-a-quiz-funnel/

### Visual Design Data

- Display participant count above start button: **+31% conversion lift**
- Progress bar visible during quiz: **+28% follow-through**
- Visual (image) questions: **+28% higher completion**
- Source: https://outgrow.co/blog/quiz-engagement-benchmarks-completion-rates

## Current State: The Hermetic Flight

The current quiz has **no email gate** -- results are shown immediately after the last question. This means every quiz completion generates zero leads.

## Recommendation

Insert an email gate screen between the final question and the results reveal. Given the sunk-cost psychology, a well-designed gate on a 7-10 question quiz should achieve 40%+ email capture.

The gate should be a single-purpose screen with:
- Archetype-themed visual treatment (maintain the mystical aesthetic)
- Value proposition: "Enter your email to reveal your archetype + begin your personalized journey"
- Single email field + submit button
- No skip option (maximizes capture; acceptable friction given strong curiosity)
- Trust signal: "Your archetype journey starts here. Unsubscribe anytime."
