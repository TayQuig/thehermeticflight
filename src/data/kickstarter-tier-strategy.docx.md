# **2\. Problems with Current Tier Structure**

## **Problem 1: Early Bird Priced Below Cost Floor**

At $44, Tier 1 offers gilded edges, a custom 3D printed box, premium card stock, a PDF guidebook, and a shout-out. Cards alone on S33 at 500 units cost \~$9.95. Add gilding (\~$5), a custom box (\~$3–$5), and production lands at $18–20 before the 3D printed box (which runs far higher per unit than rigid packaging). After Kickstarter’s \~8–10% fees, the $44 tier nets \~$38–40. Subtract $4–7 shipping, and margin evaporates. This is the highest-volume tier; scaling losses.

## **Problem 2: The $44–$100 Gap Kills Conversions**

A 127% jump from Tier 1 to Tier 2 creates a dead zone. Backers who miss the 222 early bird slots face no option under $100. Most successful tarot Kickstarters place the core offering at $55–75. You’ll lose significant volume in that gap.

## **Problem 3: Fractured Packaging Strategy**

Tier 1 offers a “customized 3D printed box.” Tier 2 offers a “book style box.” Brand guidelines position the deck with a rigid magnetic closure box. Three box types means three manufacturing pipelines, inflated costs, and diluted brand experience.

## **Problem 4: The Guidebook Is Buried**

Market research identified guidebook quality as a top pain point. Email sequences spend weeks selling the 200+ page companion book. Yet the only guidebook mention is a PDF in Tier 1\. No tier explicitly includes a physical guidebook.

## **Problem 5: “Mystery” Items Create Purchase Hesitation**

“Two exclusive 3D printed mystery major arcana cards” raises more questions than excitement. Kickstarter backers invest based on clarity. Mystery works for small bonuses; it shouldn’t be the primary differentiator.

## **Problem 6: Service Rewards Create Fulfillment Traps**

A tarot reading or aerial lesson with “must be local” limits appeal and creates open-ended scheduling obligations during your most critical post-campaign period.

## **Problem 7: Wholesale Math Doesn’t Work**

$400 for 10 decks \= $40/unit. Retailers need 50%+ margin. Wholesale should land at 40–50% of suggested retail.

## **Problem 8: Hermetic Names Hurt Scanability**

"The Veiled Nous" and "The Golden Mediator" are thematically beautiful but functionally opaque. Lead with functional descriptions; keep hermetic names as flavor text.

# **3\. Recommended Tier Structure**

Restructured tiers follow four principles: (1) every tier clears 50%+ gross margin, (2) price increases stay under 60% between adjacent tiers, (3) each tier adds tangible, clearly described value, (4) strongest differentiators appear early.
